# Voice

Status: **ready for the current runtime**.

## Positioning

This brand acts as a digital asset expertise team. The team helps organizations understand their digital asset exposure, build the frameworks and operations to address it, develop their people through continuously updated training, and operate confidently as finance moves to blockchain.

Tagline:

```text
The expertise layer for the new financial era.
```

Mission:

```text
To give every organization the expertise and readiness to see clearly and act confidently in the digital asset era.
```

## Tone

The brand should sound:

- Practitioner-led.
- Direct.
- Precise.
- Calm under pressure.
- Tool-agnostic.
- Evidence-backed.
- Senior, but not stiff.

Avoid hype. The team has real credentials, so copy should not strain to sound impressive.

## Canonical Product Names

Use these visible practice labels exactly:

- Investigations
- compliance
- institute

Rules:

- The public Investigations route is `/investigations`; keep `investigate` for existing internal component names and asset identifiers unless a component rename is explicitly requested.

- Use **the training institute** when naming the product formally.
- "Start learning" is CTA/action language, not a vertical name.
- The product model is a **flywheel**. Do not introduce door metaphors.

## Proof and Claim Discipline

Use careful attribution:

- `$38M USD identified and protected` should be framed as helped identify and protect digital assets for a customer in one divorce case, with value at the time when context allows.
- `$44.9M seized` and `$870M disrupted` should be framed as broader-team investigative outcomes.
- Do not imply the organization acted alone when the claim is a broader-team result.
- Do not name customers or partners unless the source material explicitly allows it.
- Founder/practitioner credentials can be specific: government intelligence operations, Global CCO at Kraken, Interim Global CCO at Binance, first enterprise digital asset training program at Chainalysis.

## Formatting and Case

- Nav labels are lowercase: `investigations`, `compliance`, `institute`, `who we serve`, `about`.
- Kicker labels are short mono uppercase visually, but source strings can be lowercase.
- Use plain language in headings and first paragraphs.
- Prefer periods or commas over long punctuation.
- Do not add em dashes to new copy.
- Keep FAQ answers direct and extractable for search and generative engines.

## AEO/GEO Rules

- Use clear entity definitions near the top of pages.
- Each FAQ answer should stand alone.
- Product pages should include plain-language definitions that can be lifted verbatim.
- Keep naming consistent across site, LinkedIn, press, and third-party mentions.
- Internal links should connect audience pages, product pages, and insight/resource pages.

## Banned or Risky Language

- Do not use "doors" or "entry doors" as the product model.
- Do not use "comply" as a product/vertical name.
- Do not overclaim recoveries, seizures, or customer outcomes.
- Do not call the organization a software platform.
- Do not suggest the organization is tied to one analytics tool provider.
- Do not use generic crypto hype language.
- Do not soften the practitioner authority into vague consultancy language.
